Clédat & Petitpierre's modern parade, exhibited at La Criée for the exhibition « Sculpter (faire à l'atelier) » ‘Sculpting (making in the studio)’ comes to life and wanders through public spaces.

body: >-

About

Yvan Clédat and Coco Petitpierre are sculptors, performers and directors. Since meeting in 1986, they have been exploring both exhibition spaces and stages, connecting the body and sculpture. They create ‘sculptures to be activated’ which they exhibit, inhabit or bring to life during performances. In 2013, they created La Parade moderne , a sculptural, walk-through work consisting of ten sculptures representing figures from early 20th-century modern painting: Munch, Léger, Arp, Ernst, Magritte, Malevich, etc. They are accompanied by a brass band playing the opening bars of Ravel's Boléro .
Partners: -- Les Tombées de la Nuit, Rennes -- Rennes City Centre District Authority Credits: For several years, the Norbert Fradin Foundation has contributed, through its exemplary collection, to the promotion of works that forge a living link between the traditions of the past and modernity. Since 2013, it has made its collection available free of charge, enabling the national and international dissemination of the modern parade by artists Clédat & Petitpierre.

Yvan Clédat and Coco Petitpierre are sculptors, performers and directors. Since meeting in 1986, they have been exploring both exhibition spaces and stages, connecting the body and sculpture. They create ‘sculptures to be activated’ which they exhibit, inhabit or bring to life during performances.

In 2013, they created La Parade moderne, a sculptural, walk-through work consisting of ten sculptures representing figures from early 20th-century modern painting: Munch, Léger, Arp, Ernst, Magritte, Malevich, etc. They are accompanied by a brass band playing the opening bars of Ravel's Boléro.
