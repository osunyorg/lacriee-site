La Criée centre d’art contemporain s’associe au musée national de la Marine situé dans la citadelle de Port-Louis, Morbihan. L’artiste réunionnaise Gabrielle Manglou est invitée à travailler à partir des composantes patrimoniales et muséales des lieux, notamment la richesse des liens qui unirent Port-Louis, Lorient et la Réunion au moment de la Compagnie des Indes orientales aux XVIIe et XVIIIe siècles.

About

For more than two years, Gabrielle Manglou has been working on a global project, H.O.C., hypothesis of the debossed object, which  questions material culture in Reunion, its vernacular sphere.
H.O.C. allows him to work in Rhizome. It relies on
both formal, pictorial, sociological, political,
anthropological. She plays with such notions as acculturation,
negation, value, status, loss, resilience, reparation, legitimacy, confusion, etc. sliding from one playground to another, sometimes blurring the tracks
between container and contents. » The title of the project Amarrer à l'ombre  is a reference
the lexicon of navigation but also echoes the word used
in Creole language to mean "attach", "put together". It's
precisely around this word that the artist organizes his installation
for the powder keg of the Citadel of Port Louis. 
Amarrer à l'ombre

objects, places, stories; these are as many leads as the artist explores in his work and will find
exposure to citadel. As a fine archaeologist of the past and the present,
Gabrielle Manglou makes visible subtle links, shadow zones
and the folds of history. His project of research, creation and transmission to the citadel of Port-Louis, Amarrer à l'ombre  takes place in two stages of residence from 14 to 31 October 2019, then the
8-27 June 2020. The residence will be extended by an exhibition,
open to the public from 15 July to 1 and November 2020.
Initiated since 2017 by Passerelle Art Centers in Brest and La Criée in Rennes, EXTRA Territories  develops around three areas of work: supporting emergence
art in Brittany through production and dissemination; build a
artistic project with Breton territorial actors;
Incorporating artistic emergence into an international dynamic. EXTRA Territories takes the form of residences of research and/or creative artists
and accompanied by specific mediation to strengthen
links between public, local partners, institutions and project
artistic, with a view to transmission and awareness around
the presence of artists in the territories.

production La Criée centre d’art contemporain, Rennes Musée national de la Marine, Port-Louis soutien ministère de la Culture / Drac Bretagne partenariat Passerelle centre d’art contemporain, Brest résidence à la citadelle de Port-Louis, Morbihan du 14 au 31 octobre 2019 et du 8 au 27 juin 2020 exposition à la citadelle de Port-Louis, Morbihan du 15 juillet au 1 er novembre 2020

For more than two years, Gabrielle Manglou has been working on a global project, H.O.C., hypothesis of the debossed object, which questions material culture in Reunion, its vernacular sphere. H.O.C. allows him to work in Rhizome. It relies on both formal, pictorial, sociological, political, anthropological. She plays with such notions as acculturation, negation, value, status, loss, resilience, reparation, legitimacy, confusion, etc. sliding from one playground to another, sometimes blurring the tracks between container and contents. »

The title of the project Amarrer à l'ombre is a reference the lexicon of navigation but also echoes the word used in Creole language to mean "attach", "put together". It's precisely around this word that the artist organizes his installation for the powder keg of the Citadel of Port Louis.  Amarrer à l'ombre objects, places, stories; these are as many leads as the artist explores in his work and will find exposure to citadel. As a fine archaeologist of the past and the present, Gabrielle Manglou makes visible subtle links, shadow zones and the folds of history.

His project of research, creation and transmission to the citadel of Port-Louis, Amarrer à l'ombre takes place in two stages of residence from 14 to 31 October 2019, then the 8-27 June 2020. The residence will be extended by an exhibition, open to the public from 15 July to 1and November 2020.
