About

-- Lætitia Paviani, Les onze mille vieilles , lecture‑performance
This text is the second part of a novel in anticipation series, with the very 19th century idea of having it appear in sections. Its title takes over from Guillaume Apollinaire’s Onze mille verges, which was itself inspired by the myth of the Eleven thousand virgins told by Jacques de Voragine. Both are as epic as they are comical. The first part was published in December 2017 in the insert of Pierre Paulin’s journal "Oom oom, uhm uhm" in response to his text "Boom boom, run run" and his homonymous exhibition at the Frac Île-de-France, le Plateau.
-- Brigitte Kloareg, chants et récits celtiques
Brigitte Kloareg is first and foremost a peddler of songs, her vast repertoire is anchored in the Celtic and neighboring countries, Europe in short, and also elsewhere... Passionate about languages and orality, she is a storyteller and sets poetry to music. She also tries to write occasional songs and complaints.
