As part of its itinerancy, the exhibition Daylighting : mais c'est l'eau qui parle by Euridice Zaituna Kala, initially presented at La Criée, is now installed at La Loge in Brussels. In her work, Euridice Zaituna Kala seeks out and highlights the multiplicity of narratives within historical periods that are sometimes opaque. This exhibition explores the relationship in a continuum between water, nature, and human narratives.

body: >-

About

Through a moving installation that includes industrial and blown glass,
transparent images, coloured lights, ancient seeds and multilingual
narratives, Euridice Zaituna Kala delves into the past of people and
ecosystems - such as that of the fully covered Senne River in Brussels -
that once thrived in urban areas. By attempting to establish new links
between these contexts, she challenges the dominant vision of the city,
which is purely growth-oriented.
