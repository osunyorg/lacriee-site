La Criée is repeating its project of artists' residence in primary schools in Priority Education Areas, in partnership with the City of Rennes, the Brittany Region Cultural Affairs Office (DRAC) and the Ministry of Education. The goal is to improve access to culture in educationally deprived areas and develop long-term art and culture education programmes while supporting creative projects by artists in the territories concerned.

body: >-

About

These residencies are intended as long-term ventures, in the
interests of allowing the children to try out an artistic approach,
discover the creative process leading to a work of art, and develop a
special relationship with the artists. The aim is also to provide other
forms of learning by stimulating pupils' sensory intelligence,
imagination and creativity. The projects are drawn up at the beginning
of the school year in consultation with the teaching staff; in this way
the educational objectives are jointly defined, together with a
framework of practical work and exchange that suits both artist and
pupils.
For this season La Criée has chosen artists Julie C. Fortier and
Mathilde Seguin, both committed to the principle of generating a
creative process in an educational setting.
Ma Cabane au Canada
Julie C. Fortier intends to experiment with sculpture, working from a still from Charles Chaplin's The Gold Rush .
Via meetings with the artist, workshops, film screenings and the idea
of "moving through the screen", students will be encouraged to go into
the image-making process in greater detail.
