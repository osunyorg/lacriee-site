 A l'occasion de son exposition à La Criée centre d'art contemporain, Euridice Zaituna Kala invite Manthia Diawara a présenté son film AI: African Intelligence.
Autorisé à assister à un Ndeup, une cérémonie de guérison spirituelle pratiquée par les peuples Lebou au Sénégal, le cinéaste et écrivain Manthia Diawara - avec la contribution de scientifiques et d'universitaires - explore la relation entre les rituels africains de possession dans les villages de pêcheurs traditionnels de la côte atlantique du Sénégal et l’émergence de nouvelles frontières technologiques, connues sous le nom d’intelligence artificielle. Dans cette confluence de la tradition et de la modernité, il s’interroge sur la manière dont nous pouvons évoluer vers une approche plus humaine et spirituelle de ces algorithmes.

About







AI: African Intelligence is a project by Manthia Diawara for the scientific residency program,
initiated by GLUON. We invited researchers Tarek Besold (Chief
Behavioral Officer, Alpha Health, Telefonica) and Raoul Frese (Professor
of Biophysics, Faculty of Sciences, Physics and Astronomy, VU
Amsterdam) for a residency in Manthia Diawara's studio in Yene, near
Dakar, Senegal.For three years, they engaged in various discussions aimed at challenging
the superiority of science over the arts in the 20th century, while also
seeking to contest the idea that science and technology evolve
inevitably and autonomously, without the possibility of being questioned
(known as technological and scientific determinism). For them, artists
can play a role by experimentally bringing creative, critical, and
societal ideas.

Manthia Diawara was born in Mali, West Africa. He is a Distinguished
Professor of Comparative Literature and Film at New York University.
Diawara is a prolific writer and filmmaker. His essays on art, cinema,
and politics have appeared in the New York Times Magazine, LA Times, Libération, Mediapart et Artforum. He has published several books on African and African-American cinema. Notable films by Diawara include: An Opera of the World (2017), Negritude: A Dialogue between Soyinka and Senghor (2016), Édouard Glissant, One World in Relation (2010), Maison Tropicale (2008) et Rouch in Reverse (1995).

AI: African Intelligence is a project by Manthia Diawara for the scientific residency program, initiated by GLUON. We invited researchers Tarek Besold (Chief Behavioral Officer, Alpha Health, Telefonica) and Raoul Frese (Professor of Biophysics, Faculty of Sciences, Physics and Astronomy, VU Amsterdam) for a residency in Manthia Diawara's studio in Yene, near Dakar, Senegal.
For three years, they engaged in various discussions aimed at challenging the superiority of science over the arts in the 20th century, while also seeking to contest the idea that science and technology evolve inevitably and autonomously, without the possibility of being questioned (known as technological and scientific determinism). For them, artists can play a role by experimentally bringing creative, critical, and societal ideas.

Manthia Diawara was born in Mali, West Africa. He is a Distinguished Professor of Comparative Literature and Film at New York University. Diawara is a prolific writer and filmmaker. His essays on art, cinema, and politics have appeared in the New York Times Magazine, LA Times, Libération, Mediapart et Artforum. He has published several books on African and African-American cinema. Notable films by Diawara include: An Opera of the World (2017), Negritude: A Dialogue between Soyinka and Senghor (2016), Édouard Glissant, One World in Relation (2010), Maison Tropicale (2008) et Rouch in Reverse (1995).
