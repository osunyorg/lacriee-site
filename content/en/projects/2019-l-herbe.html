Evariste Richer

body: >-


plaster, selenite, 29 x 12 x 10 cm





L’herbe  (Grass) refers to the green selenite (gypsum) deposited in the hollow of a plaster-molded hand. The work proposes a dialogue between what is available (a production of nature) and what makes art (the sculptor’s primary tool). It is part of a series where Evariste Richer molds each time differently a hand, matching the size of the stones he collects, and in which the dust from his workshop settles.



courtesy of the artist and Meessen De Clercq, Brussels
