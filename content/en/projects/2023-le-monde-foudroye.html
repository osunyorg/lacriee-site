body: >-


watercolour on paper140 × 300 cm


Le monde foudroyé is a large-scale watercolor that depicts a map of the
world, with color nuances revealing the number of lightning strikes per
square kilometer per year. The accompanying color chart resembles that
of the light spectrum. On the map, the areas in red and black correspond
to the regions most heavily affected by lightning, particularly the
Democratic Republic of Congo in Central Africa and Venezuela in South
America. The fine watercolor layers appear as a colored nebula, a moiré
camouflage, through which the world's topography and its fires are revealed.
