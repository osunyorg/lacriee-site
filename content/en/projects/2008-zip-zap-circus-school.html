Zip Zap Circus Schoo l is composed of
three wooden and fabric modules, offering an imaginary space between the
European and African discourses on architecture. Two unfinished projects are articulated in the Ferreira facility
: a villa project designed in 1912 for the Kröller Müller family by
Modernist architect Mies van der Rohe with his particular technique
"repetition" by wooden and fabric structures that could
be moved to the planned site of a park in Holland. Other project
is the one designed in the 1990s by the architect Mozambican Pancho Guedes for a circus school for the South African community of Cape Town.

Ângela Ferreira, Zip Zap Circus School , École des beaux-arts de Rennes, 2000 - 2002
vue de l'exposition Hard Rain Show , La Criée centre d'art contemporain et l'École des beaux-arts de Rennes, 2009,
photo : Benoît Mauras
