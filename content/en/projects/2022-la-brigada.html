body: >-


video, colour, sound, 12 min 28 sec screen, metal structure, 24 shoe-shine boxes collected in La Paz, Bolivia, variable dimensions


Conducted in Bolivia, the project La Brigada is carried out with street shoe shiners in La Paz. Both noticeable and nomadic in the city, they are the guardians of a
'small trade' that is precarious and despised. Often masked to avoid
recognition, they try to catch the attention of passersby by tapping on
small, colorful wooden boxes. Attentive and sensitive to their
conditions, Bertille Bak invited them to transform their mundane
gestures into "those of a brigade defending the polished shoe, powerful
and respectable." She built with the collective a new form of protest,
both poetic and political. This shift from reality to fiction is
amplified by the presence of their personalized and colorful work tools.
Bertille Bak has gathered the shoe shiners' boxes into a collection of
ethnographic objects, highlighting the humanity and uniqueness of each
of these invisible individuals. Production:
-- French Embassy in Bolivia
-- La Criée Contemporary Art Center
